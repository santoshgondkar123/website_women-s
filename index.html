why they inspire us

✦ ✦ ✦

These women inspire millions of people to follow their dreams, work hard, and contribute to society. Their courage, resilience, and vision remind us that no dream is too big and no obstacle too great. They are living proof that one person can ignite change and leave an indelible mark on the world.

Indira Gandhi

1917 – 1984 · first & only woman PM
  • India's first and to date only female Prime Minister
  • Led India to victory in 1971 war; created Bangladesh
  • Nationalized banks & abolished privy purses
  • Known as the "Iron Lady of India"

Indira Priyadarshini Gandhi served as Prime Minister from 1966 to 1977 and again from 1980 until her assassination. A central figure of the Indian National Congress, she remains a symbol of strong leadership.

Kalpana Chawla

1962 – 2003 · astronaut · first Indian woman in space
  • First Indian-born woman to go to space (STS-87, 1997)
  • Aerospace engineer, PhD from University of Colorado
  • Performed experiments on microgravity
  • Died in Columbia disaster, but inspired millions

"The path from dreams to success does exist." Kalpana Chawla's journey from Karnal to the stars embodies courage and curiosity.

Mary Kom

b. 1982 · "Magnificent Mary"
  • Six-time World Amateur Boxing champion
  • Only woman boxer to win a medal in each of the six world championships
  • Bronze medal at 2012 London Olympics
  • Padma Bhushan, Rajya Sabha member

Mary Kom rose from a small village in Manipur to become a global boxing icon – a symbol of resilience and mother of three.

Lata Mangeshkar

1929 – 2022 · the Nightingale of India
  • Recorded songs in over 36 languages
  • Guinness record for most studio recordings (1948–2010s)
  • Bharat Ratna, France's highest civilian award
  • Voice of generations – "Ae Mere Watan Ke Logon"

For seven decades, Lata Mangeshkar's voice defined Indian melody. She remains immortal through her timeless songs.

Savitribai Phule

1831 – 1897 · first female teacher of India
  • India's first woman teacher (1848) along with husband Jyotirao
  • Pioneer of women's rights & caste reform
  • Opened shelter for widows & prevented infanticide
  • Wrote poetry to inspire education for all

Savitribai Phule fought against caste and patriarchy to light the lamp of education for girls. Her legacy lives in every classroom.
